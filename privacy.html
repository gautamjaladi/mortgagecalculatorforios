Location may be sent to third-party services only to return those results. - You can use the App without granting location or notification permissions (some features may be limited). ## 2) Information you provide (stored on your device) The App lets you enter and save information such as: - Property details (e.g., address or nickname, price, taxes, HOA, insurance) - Loan details (e.g., down payment, rate, term, PMI assumptions) - Budget/income assumptions you choose to enter (e.g., income, debts, maintenance/utilities assumptions) - Preferences (e.g., currency, settings) This information is saved locally on your device to power calculations, insights, and PDF exports. ## 3) Location information (optional) If you grant location permission (When In Use): - We access your approximate or precise location while the App is in use to provide: - local weather - regional benchmarks and context (e.g., area median income, area averages), based on your region **How it’s used:** The App may send your location (latitude/longitude) to third-party services to fetch weather and regional lookup results. We do not use location for advertising or tracking. You can change location permissions anytime in iOS Settings. If you deny location access, you can still use the App, but local features may be unavailable or require manual input. ## 4) Notifications (optional) If you enable notifications: - The App may send notifications you opt into (e.g., reminders or alerts you configure). - You can turn notifications off anytime in iOS Settings. ## 5) Network requests and third-party services The App fetches certain information from third-party or public data sources to provide features like weather, benchmarks, and headlines. Depending on what you use, requests may include: - Your approximate location or coordinates (if you allow location) - A region identifier (such as ZIP/city/state) derived from your location or entered by you - General queries used to fetch public headlines Third-party services may receive standard request information (such as IP address and user agent) as part of normal internet communication. Their handling of that information is governed by their own privacy policies. **Examples of sources may include (edit to match your actual sources):** - Weather data provider (e.g., Open-Meteo) - Public economic/housing datasets (e.g., U.S. Census, FRED) - Regional mapping/lookup services (e.g., FCC) - Public mortgage/credit datasets (e.g., FFIEC/HMDA or similar public endpoints) - News feeds (e.g., Google News RSS) ## 6) Analytics and tracking - We do not use advertising tracking in the App. - If we add analytics or crash reporting tools in the future, we will update this policy and App Store privacy disclosures accordingly. ## 7) Reports and exports (PDF) You may export a PDF report from the App. Exported reports may include the property/loan inputs and insights shown in the App for the selected property.
